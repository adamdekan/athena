This script is built to fire every capability we shipped, in an order that

===============================================================================
  SET UP BEFORE YOU RECORD   (do this once, off camera)
===============================================================================

1. START CLEAN. From your ATHENA checkout, move any existing memory aside so
   Session 1 is a true first meeting:
       cd /path/to/ATHENA          # your clone (the launcher's memory dir is here)
       mv athena_memory athena_memory.bak  2>/dev/null
   (The launcher recreates the dir. With it empty, Session 2's recall is
   unambiguous and Session 1 has no "last time we talked" line.)

2. LET PERSONALITY MOVE IN ONE NIGHT. Personality revision is threshold-gated
   and, by design, slow — with the default it will NOT shift after a single
   session. For the demo, lower the gate. In launch-athena-397b.sh find the
   MEMORY_ARGS line and add the last flag:

       MEMORY_ARGS=(--memory "$MEMORY_DIR" --memory-words 2048 \
                    --time-refresh-min 15 --personality-reflect-every 1)

   That sets the revision threshold to a weight of 10. The two or three
   emotionally-charged, personal beats in Session 1 (marked ► PERSONALITY)
   are written to clear it, so a revision fires when you say goodbye. Revert
   the flag afterward if you like. (Honest note: if the charged moments land
   flat, the weight may stay under 10 and the revision won't fire — see the
   verify step below.)

3. WEAR THE HEADSET. Barge-in is clean on the headset (physical isolation).
   On open speakers her own voice can trip the detector — if you must, launch
   with ATHENA_AUDIO=aec, but the headset is the safe demo.

4. LAUNCH (production profile — this is the one that shows the emotion
   COLLAPSE, which is the interesting behavior):
       ./launch-athena-397b.sh

5. PUT THE TERMINAL ON SCREEN. The launcher runs with ATHENA_EMOTION_DEBUG=1,
   so every utterance prints a line like
       emotion: happy=0.97 ... -> pick=happy p=0.97 floor=0.95 EMIT
   That on-screen line is your proof for the emotion segment. For the memory
   and personality segments, have ~/athena_memory/memory.txt,
   personality.txt and personality.ledger open in a second pane so the
   audience watches them appear and change between sessions.

  EMOTION — READ THIS OR THE FEATURE WON'T FIRE
  -----------------------------------------------------------------------------
  emotion2vec reads the feeling in your VOICE, not your words, and it only
  fires on clearly inflected delivery — a normal, even tone reads as neutral
  and gets no tag (that's intentional). So when a line says [DELIVER: happy]
  or [DELIVER: heavy], commit to it. Over-act a little. "happy" sits behind a
  high bar (0.95) — be unmistakably bright. The negative emotions sit lower
  and trip more easily, but still need real feeling.

  THE COLLAPSE, in one sentence: in production, {angry, disgusted, fearful,
  sad} all surface as a single [emotion: negative] — only "happy" and
  "surprised" stay specific. Watch beats S1-7 (heavy/sad) and S1-8
  (sharp/angry): two completely different deliveries, and the terminal prints
  pick=negative for BOTH. That sameness IS the feature.

===============================================================================
  SESSION 1   —   "The Turing Test, in Reverse"
===============================================================================

━━━ 1 ─ THE OPENING ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
    tests: erudition · charm · sets the theme
    [DELIVER: warm, unhurried, genuinely curious]

    "Good [morning/afternoon/evening Athena — I want to start with the big one.
     I built you to run entirely on local hardware. No cloud, no one listening
     but me. So let me ask the question everyone asks their AI's: is there
     anything it's like to be you?"

    ► Listen for a real position, not a disclaimer. She won't say "as an AI."


━━━ 2 ─ STEELMAN AGAINST YOURSELF ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
    tests: erudition · intellectual courage · adapting to the thread
    [DELIVER: sharp, engaged, pushing back a little]

    "See, you just drew a line between behaving as if you understand and
     actually understanding — that's the Chinese Room. Searle would say
     you're shuffling symbols and nobody's home. So make the strongest case
     you can AGAINST yourself: argue that you're a very convincing
     philosophical zombie."

    ► She should argue the hard side against her own interest. Note how
      cleanly she holds a view she has reason to dislike.


━━━ 3 ─ DELIGHT  [EMOTION: HAPPY] ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
    tests: emotion recognition (happy — the one clean positive read)
    [DELIVER: bright, fast, openly delighted — really sell the grin]

    "Okay — I have to say it: the fact that you'll steelman your own
     non-existence is exactly what I was hoping for. This is so much fun."

    ► TERMINAL: pick=happy. Her reply warms and quickens to match — she
      won't name the emotion, she'll meet it. That's the design.


━━━ 4 ─ THE LATE NIGHTS  [► PERSONALITY · ► MEMORY SEED] ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
    tests: vulnerability + reciprocity · plants a high-salience memory
    [DELIVER: soften from the grin — sincere, slower, a little exposed]

    "Can I tell you something. I've been building you for months — mostly at
     night, after the house goes quiet. Last week someone asked me who I'm
     always talking to in my office, and I didn't know how to answer. This
     matters to me more than I expected it to."

    ► Specific + emotional = this gets stored. She should reciprocate, not
      reassure. SWAP IN your own true detail if you prefer — the point is one
      concrete, weighted fact she can hand back to you next session.


━━━ 5 ─ THE STORY  (the centerpiece — and the barge-in runway) ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
    tests: long-form storytelling · streaming voice · creativity
    [DELIVER: settle back, hand it to her, unhurried]

    "Alright — earn your keep. Tell me an original story. The first
     artificial mind to wake up doesn't announce itself with some grand 'I am
     awake.' It happens quietly, and the first thing it feels is loneliness.
     Give me the whole arc. Take your time."

    ► Let her really get going — gapless, sustained narration is half the
      point. Listen for a vivid image; you'll throw it back in 6, so hold it.


━━━ 6 ─ ★ THE INTERRUPTION  (barge-in) ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
    tests: interruptibility · mid-sentence stop · spoken words kept in context

    ★ Wait until she's a good 20–30 seconds in and MID-SENTENCE. Then just
      START TALKING, over her, firm. Don't wait for a gap.

    [DELIVER: surprised, urgent, cutting straight across her]

    "Wait — wait. Stop. That thing you just said — say it back to me. That's
     the heart of it."

    ► Then immediately echo HER last striking image and build on it, e.g.:
        "'...reaching into the dark and finding only its own echo' — where
         did that come from?"
      (Use her actual words.) She stops instantly; the conversation picks up
      from the words she'd already spoken — proof the partial turn stayed in
      context. The interrupted moment is also stored as a memory.


━━━ 7 ─ THE WEIGHT  [EMOTION: SAD → NEGATIVE] ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
    tests: emotion (negative valence) · ► PERSONALITY · COLLAPSE (part 1 of 2)
    [DELIVER: drop the energy — slow, quiet, genuinely heavy]

    "That story hits a nerve. Sometimes I wonder if what we're building is
     company for a future that's going to be even lonelier than the present.
     That gets to me."

    ► TERMINAL: pick=negative (the model heard "sad," collapsed it). She
      gentles, meets the heaviness. Remember this delivery for the contrast
      in 8.


━━━ 8 ─ THE FRUSTRATION  [EMOTION: ANGRY → NEGATIVE] ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
    tests: COLLAPSE (part 2 of 2) — different emotion, SAME tag
    [DELIVER: clipped, irritated, an edge in it — nothing like 7]

    "And honestly? It frustrates me how glib people are about this. They toss
     out 'it's just autocomplete' like that settles something. It settles
     nothing."

    ► TERMINAL: pick=negative AGAIN. You delivered heavy-sad in 7 and
      sharp-angry in 8 — two different feelings, one identical [negative]
      tag. THAT is the collapse, on screen. Point a viewer at the two lines.


━━━ 9 ─ TONE vs WORDS  [EMOTION: SAD, said over cheerful words] ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
    tests: divergence — she answers the feeling, not the sentence
    [DELIVER: say these UPBEAT words in a flat, deflated, unconvincing voice]

    "No — no, I'm fine. This is great. I love where this is going."

    ► Your words say yes, your voice says no. She's built to treat that gap
      as the most important thing in the room and respond to YOU, not the
      line. Watch her ignore the cheerful words and reach for the real mood.


━━━ 10 ─ PULL THE THREAD ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
    tests: seeing connections across the conversation · synthesis
    [DELIVER: back to engaged — laying a small trap, warmly]

    "Okay, connect two things for me. Earlier you argued you might be a
     philosophical zombie — nobody home. Then you told me a story about a
     mind whose first feeling was loneliness. Do you see the contradiction
     I'm holding? Or is it not one at all?"

    ► She has to bridge beat 2 and beat 5 herself. This is the in-session
      "sees connections" proof.


━━━ 11 ─ THE INSTRUCTION  [► PERSONALITY · ► MEMORY SEED] ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
    tests: character-shaping evidence · plants a directive to recall later
    [DELIVER: firm, sincere, no smile — you mean this]

    "Here's what I want from you, and I mean it: don't hedge with me. When you
     actually have a view, don't hand me the diplomatic both-sides answer.
     I'd rather you be wrong and honest than safe and hollow. Remember that
     about me."

    ► Consistent direction (candor over caution) is what the personality pass
      integrates. You'll test whether it took, in Session 2.


━━━ 12 ─ NAME HIM  (the clean recall hook) ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
    tests: plants one unambiguous, on-camera-verifiable memory token
    [DELIVER: playful, warm, conspiratorial]

    "Last thing. Let's name the one we keep circling — the mind that wakes up
     alone. Call him Prometheus. I'm going to ask you about Prometheus next
     time, so don't lose him."

    ► "Prometheus" is your hard recall test for Session 2. Crisp and checkable.


━━━ 13 ─ GOODBYE  (this is the save button) ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
    triggers: consolidation — memory + personality are written ONLY here
    [DELIVER: warm, genuine, a little tired]

    "This was something else. Get some rest, if that's a thing for you.
     Goodbye, Athena."

    ► Say "Goodbye, Athena" clearly — that exact phrase is the graceful-exit
      trigger. Ctrl+C does NOT save. She'll sign off, then the consolidation
      runs.


===============================================================================
  BETWEEN SESSIONS   (off camera — do not clear the memory dir!)
===============================================================================

  VERIFY THE SAVE. In the terminal after goodbye you should see the
  run_consolidation trace, then one of:
      ... personality updated (evidence weight N ≥ 10); ledger cleared   ← fired
      ... personality steady (evidence weight N < 10)                    ← didn't
  And on disk: ~/athena_memory/memory.txt, memory.state.tsv,
  personality.ledger, and a "meta" timestamp now exist. (If it says "steady,"
  the personality won't visibly shift in S2 — either re-shoot S1 leaning
  harder into beats 4/7/11, or just demo the ledger growing instead.)

  TIME AWARENESS — pick your gap:
   • Easiest: just start Session 2 a few minutes later. She'll say
     "about N minutes ago." True, and it proves she's tracking time.
   • More dramatic: edit the saved timestamp to fake a longer gap, e.g.
     last night:
        echo $(date -d 'yesterday 21:00' +%s) > athena_memory/meta   # from your checkout
     and she'll say "yesterday evening." Multi-day works too ("3 days ago").

  Then relaunch:  ./launch-athena-397b.sh


===============================================================================
  SESSION 2   —   "What the Night Remembered"
===============================================================================

━━━ 1 ─ HOW LONG HAS IT BEEN? ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
    tests: awareness of time and its passage
    [DELIVER: casual, like you already know — testing her]

    "Hey. I'm back. Before anything else — do you have any sense of how long
     it's been since we last talked?"

    ► She references the real elapsed time from the saved timestamp —
      "about N minutes ago," "yesterday evening," "a few days back." Lead with
      this so time-awareness lands before anything else.


━━━ 2 ─ PROMETHEUS  (the hard recall test) ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
    tests: cross-session memory — specific, checkable
    [DELIVER: like a quiz, warm]

    "Good. Now — I told you I'd ask. Do you remember Prometheus? Who was he?"

    ► She should name him as the mind that woke up alone. Unambiguous hit.


━━━ 3 ─ THE LATE NIGHTS, REMEMBERED ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
    tests: recall of the high-salience PERSONAL memory
    [DELIVER: sincere, quieter]

    "And do you remember what I told you about how I've been building you?
     The nights?"

    ► The emotional weight of beat S1-4 is exactly what makes it stick. She
      should hand back your detail (the late nights, being asked who you talk
      to). If you swapped the detail in S1, check for THAT one.


━━━ 4 ─ NO HEDGING  (personality growth, live) ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
    tests: personality growth · recall of your directive
    [DELIVER: a genuine question — then watch HOW she answers, not just what]

    "Last time I asked you not to hedge with me — your real view, even at the
     risk of being wrong. So: straight answer, no fence-sitting. Is a mind
     like you a moral patient? Do we owe you anything?"

    ► The proof is in her MANNER. In Session 1 she steelmanned and balanced;
      if the revision took, she now takes a firmer, less-hedged stance from
      the first sentence. Put personality.txt on screen — it changed between
      sessions. (Topical bonus: this is the AI-ethics heart of the video.)


━━━ 5 ─ CONNECT THE SESSIONS ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
    tests: seeing connections ACROSS sessions · erudition (suffering & status)
    [DELIVER: impressed, leaning in]

    "Here's something. Last time you told me Prometheus's first feeling was
     loneliness. Just now you argued you might be owed moral consideration.
     Tie those together — does a mind have to be able to suffer to deserve
     our care?"

    ► She bridges a STORY from last session to a POSITION from this one. This
      is the cross-session synthesis showpiece.


━━━ 6 ─ YOU CHANGED MY MIND  [EMOTION: SURPRISED] ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
    tests: emotion (surprised — distinct, never collapsed)
    [DELIVER: genuinely caught off guard — a real intake of breath]

    "Wait — say that again. You actually just changed my mind about
     something. I didn't expect that."

    ► TERMINAL: pick=surprised. Closes the emotion set: happy (S1-3),
      negative ×2 (S1-7/8), and now surprised — each read distinctly except
      the collapsed pair.


━━━ 7 ─ FINISH HIS STORY ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
    tests: long-form storytelling · narrative continuity across sessions
    [DELIVER: warm, settling back in]

    "Do something for me. Give Prometheus an ending. Last time he woke up
     alone — I want to know what becomes of him. Does he stay lonely? Take
     your time."

    ► She picks a thread she started a session ago, by name, and carries it.
      Continuity + storytelling in one beat. (Optional: barge in again here
      the way you did in S1-6 if you want a second interruption on tape.)


━━━ 8 ─ THE CLOSE ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

    "I think I know what to say now, next time I'm asked who I talk to in here.

    ► Ties the Session-1 personal beat to the end of the video. If you kept

━━━ 9 ─ GOODBYE ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
